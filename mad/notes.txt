  The new logging is useful because it shows why.

  For subset_sac_unfrozen, the policy learns, but the encoder drifts badly:

  - latent_subset_to_full_mse grows from about 0.0002 to 0.341
  - latent_subset_to_full_cos falls from about 0.9998 to 0.698
  - encoder_grad_norm explodes from 0.34 to 57k
  - critic_loss grows from 0.43 to 1294

  See train_1.csv (/home/medcvr/Desktop/hamza/multicam-vit/MultiModalViT/mad/seq_runs/
  button_press_seq_20260406_002757/subset_sac_unfrozen/logs/train_1.csv).

  Interpretation: unfrozen RL is solving the task partly by rewriting the pretrained latent space.
  That helps full-view control, but it destroys the subset/full alignment you trained the ViT for.
  That is the most likely reason single-camera success is still mediocre.

  For subset_sac_frozen, the opposite happens:

  - latent alignment stays excellent: MSE stays around 0.001, cosine around 0.999
  - losses stay much more bounded
  - but eval success never gets off the floor

  See train_1.csv (/home/medcvr/Desktop/hamza/multicam-vit/MultiModalViT/mad/seq_runs/
  button_press_seq_20260406_002757/subset_sac_frozen/logs/train_1.csv).

  Interpretation: the frozen latent is stable, but this policy setup is not extracting a successful
  controller from it under the current hyperparameters.

  What To Improve Next
  The next runs I would do are:

  - Run frozen and unfrozen with the same batch size, preferably 32 or 64. Right now the comparison
    is confounded.
  - Add vit_latent_full_sac to the sequence. It may be that subset-SAC on a frozen ViT is too hard,
    while plain full-latent SAC would learn.
  - Lower vit_encoder_lr for unfrozen runs. Try 1e-6 or 3e-6. 1e-5 is clearly causing encoder
    drift.
  - Add an unfreeze schedule: keep the ViT frozen for the first 25k-50k steps, then unfreeze.
  - Add encoder regularization if unfrozen: keep the RL-finetuned latent close to the initial
    pretrained latent or preserve subset/full alignment during RL. The current logs strongly
    suggest this is needed.
  - Evaluate more often early, like every 10k. The MAD baseline solved the task very early, so
    waiting 25k hides useful learning differences.
